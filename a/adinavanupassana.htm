Buddhist Dictionary by NYANATILOKA

  • ādīnavānupassanā-ñāna

'knowledge consisting in contemplation of misery', 

is one of the 8 kinds of insight (vipassanā) that form the 'purification of the knowledge and vision of the path-progress (s. visuddhi, VI. 4). 

It is further one of the 18 chief kinds of insight (s. vipassanā).
