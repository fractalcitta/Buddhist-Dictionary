  • asekha

(lit.: 'not-learner'; s. sekha), a disciple 'perfected in training', one beyond training, an adept.

This is a name for the Arahat, the Holy One (s. ariya-puggala), since he has reached the perfection in higher moral training, higher mind training and higher wisdom training (s. sikkhƒÅ) and needs no longer to train himself therein.
