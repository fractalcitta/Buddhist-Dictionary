BUDDHIST DICTIONARY - B -

  • bahula-kamma: 'habitual karma': s. karma.
  • bala
  • balance of mental faculties: indriya samatta.
  • bases: The 12 of the perceptual process: āyatana.
  • beautiful: sobhana.
  • beauty, deliverance through the perception of: cf. vimokkha (II. 3) To hold for beautiful or pure (subha) what is impure (asubha), is one of the 4 perversions (s. vipallāsa).
  • behaviour, morality consisting in good: abhisamācārikasīla.
  • being, living: satta; further s. puggala. - Belief in eternal personality: bhava-ditthi (s. ditthi), sassata-ditthi.
  • beings, The 9 worlds of: sattāvāsa.
  • belief, blind: s. indriya-samatta.
  • bhangānupassanā-ñāna: 'knowledge consisting in contemplation of dissolution' (of all forms of existence), is one kind of insight: s. visuddhi (VI, 2).
  • bhava
  • bhāva
  • bhava-ditthi: 'belief in being' (eternal personality); s. sassataditthi, ditthi.
  • bhāvanā
  • bhāvanā-bala: s. patisankhāna-bala.
  • bhāvanā-maya-paññā: wisdom based on mental development'; s. paññā
  • bhavanga-citta: see bhavanga-sota
  • bhavanga-santāna: 'continuity of subconsciousness'; s. santāna
  • bhavanga-sota and bhavanga-citta
  • bhava-tanhā: 'craving for (eternal) existence'; s. tanhā.
  • bhavāsava: 'canker of existence'; s. āsava.
  • bhayatupatthāna-ñāna
  • bhikkhu
  • bhojane mattaññutā
  • biases: s. āsava.
  • birth process: upapatti-bhava: s. bhava. Further s. patisandhi, jāti.
  • bodhi
  • bodhipakkhiya-dhammā
  • Bodhisatta
  • bodily action (wholesome or unwholesome); s. karma, karma formations - Right b.a. = sammā-kammanta; s. magga.
  • bodily postures, the 4: iriyā-patha.
  • body: kāya. Contemplation on the b. is one of the 4 satipatthāna.
  • body-witness: kāya-sakkhi.
  • bojjhanga
  • bondages, mental: cetaso vinibandha.
  • bonds, the 4: yoga.
  • both-ways liberated, s. ubhato-bhāga-vimutta, ariyapuggala B. 4.
  • boundless consciousness (and b. space), Sphere of: s. jhāna 5, 6.
  • brahma-cariya
  • brahma-kāyika-deva
  • brahma-loka
  • brahma-vihāra
  • breathing, mindfulness of in-and-out-breathing ānāpānasati.
  • Buddha: s. sammā-sambodhi.
  • buddhānussati: 'recollection of the Enlightened One'; s. anussati.
  • Buddha-sāsana: s. sāsana.
