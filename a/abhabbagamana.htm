• abhabbāgamana

'incapable of progressing'. 

"Those beings 

  • who are obstructed by their evil actions (kamma, s. karma), 
  • by their defilements (kilesa), 
  • by the result of their evil actions (s. vipāka), 
  • or who are devoid of faith, 
  • energy and 
  • knowledge, and 
  • unable to enter the right path and reach perfection in wholesome things, 

all those are said to be incapable of progressing" (Pug. 13). 

According to Commentary the 'evil actions' denote the 5 heinous deeds with immediate result (ānantarika-kamma), whilst the 'defilements' refer to the 'evil views with fixed destiny' (niyata-micchā-ditthi; s. ditthi).
